MacLean: Sums

Sums

MacLean provides a family of functors which construct sums. A sum of (x:5) and (y:7) is constructed by picking one and injecting it with one of the the encodings 

inj0: 5 -> 12 
      z = x + 7
inj1: 7 -> 12 
      z = y
Decoding a sum requires determining which injector was used to construct it, along with extracting the value injected. This is usually done with a case construction: 
case z of
  0 : 11..7 => z - 7 : 5
  1 : 6..0 => z : 7
The notation 5 + 7 is commonly used to represent the sum. Note that the term denotes the two constructing injections, as well as the decoder. The actual type targetted by the injections is just \(12\) which is also the domain of the decoder.
